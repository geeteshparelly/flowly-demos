About Us

The story behind the baked goods

Why I Started Baking

A few years ago, I was diagnosed with multiple food sensitivities. Gluten, dairy, eggs — suddenly, most of the foods I loved were off the table. Bakeries became places I'd walk past, not into. Birthday parties meant watching everyone else eat cake.

So I started experimenting in my kitchen. Dozens of failed batches. Cookies that crumbled into dust. Brownies that could double as bricks. But eventually, something clicked. I figured out how to make treats that tasted like the real thing — no compromise, no sadness.

"If I wouldn't eat it myself, I won't sell it to you. That's the rule."

Jane's Bountiful Pantry started at my local farmers market with three products and a folding table. Now I'm at multiple markets every week, and I've shipped treats across Texas. But the mission hasn't changed: make food that people with dietary restrictions can actually enjoy — not just tolerate.

What Makes Us Different

I'm not a big bakery. I don't have a factory or a team of bakers. Every item is made by hand, in small batches, in my dedicated gluten-free kitchen. That means I can control exactly what goes into each product.

No mystery ingredients. No "natural flavors" hiding dairy. No shared equipment with wheat products. When I say something is gluten-free and vegan, I mean it — because I'm eating the same thing you are.
